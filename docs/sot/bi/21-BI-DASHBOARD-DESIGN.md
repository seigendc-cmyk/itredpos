# SCI POS Business Intelligence Dashboard Design

## Purpose

The Business Intelligence Dashboard is the primary decision-making interface of SCI POS.

It must transform large volumes of operational data into simple, understandable business intelligence.

The dashboard must be designed for ordinary business owners, branch managers, warehouse supervisors, cashiers, and executives.

The objective is to make intelligence easy to understand and easy to act upon.

## Core Design Principle

The dashboard must answer:

- What happened?
- What is happening now?
- What is at risk?
- What will likely happen next?
- What action should be taken?

Users should not need accounting or analytics expertise to understand the dashboard.

## Dashboard Structure

The Business Intelligence section shall behave like a digital business intelligence book.

The user can:

- Search any topic
- Open any intelligence chapter
- Apply filters
- Compare periods
- Compare branches
- Compare staff
- Compare suppliers
- Investigate risk

## Landing Dashboard

The landing page must provide a Business Health Overview.

Widgets include:

### Business Health Score

Overall score calculated from:

- Profit Protection
- Stock Integrity
- Cash Control
- Staff Risk
- Customer Retention
- Delivery Performance

### Protected Profit

Displays:

- Gross Profit
- Profit Leakage
- Protected Profit

### Critical Alerts

Displays:

- Open Critical Alerts
- High-Risk Events
- Unresolved Investigations

### Top Risks

Displays:

- Stock Risk
- Cash Risk
- Staff Risk
- Supplier Risk
- Delivery Risk

### Business Recommendations

Displays:

- Immediate Actions
- Recommended Reviews
- Priority Tasks

## Intelligence Chapters

The BI page shall contain:

### Dashboard

Executive overview.

### Stock Integrity

Stock accuracy and variance analysis.

### Stocktake Intelligence

Count planning and count performance.

### Theft Detection

Loss detection and risk monitoring.

### COGS Intelligence

Cost and margin analysis.

### Profit Protection

Profit preservation and leakage analysis.

### Cash Control

Cash accountability and variance analysis.

### Staff Intelligence

Performance and risk monitoring.

### Sales Intelligence

Sales performance and forecasting.

### Purchase Intelligence

Purchasing discipline and demand planning.

### Supplier Intelligence

Supplier performance and reliability.

### Delivery Intelligence

Fulfilment performance and delivery control.

### Customer Intelligence

Retention, churn, and customer value.

### Branch Intelligence

Multi-branch comparison and health analysis.

### Risk Centre

Unified risk management.

### Alerts Centre

Open and historical alerts.

### Recommendation Centre

Recommended actions and decisions.

## Global Filters

All BI pages must support:

- Date Range
- Branch
- Warehouse
- Product Category
- Product
- Staff Member
- Supplier
- Delivery Driver
- Customer
- Risk Level

Filters must work across all BI chapters.

## Search Engine

Users must be able to search in free order.

Examples:

"products with highest variance"

"cash shortages this month"

"supplier cost increases"

"branches losing profit"

"customers at risk of churn"

Search must return relevant BI views.

## Visual Design

The dashboard must use:

- Sharp professional layouts
- Square-edge panels
- Business-focused colors
- High information density
- Mobile responsiveness
- Large-screen optimization

Avoid excessive decoration.

Prioritize clarity and actionability.

## Role-Based Views

### Owner

Full BI access.

### Branch Manager

Branch-specific intelligence.

### Warehouse Manager

Inventory-focused intelligence.

### Cashier

Personal performance and compliance intelligence.

### Delivery Manager

Delivery performance and fulfilment intelligence.

## Strategic Principle

The Business Intelligence Dashboard is not a reporting screen.

It is the decision-making centre of SCI POS.

Every page must help users identify risk, protect profit, improve control, and make better business decisions.
