# SCI POS Business Intelligence COGS Intelligence

## Purpose

COGS means Cost of Goods Sold.

COGS Intelligence protects the vendor from selling products without understanding true cost, true margin, and true profit.

Many businesses appear to be selling well while quietly losing money because purchase costs increase, selling prices remain unchanged, discounts are uncontrolled, stock losses are ignored, and landed costs are not included.

SCI POS must make COGS visible, understandable, and actionable.

## Core Principle

A sale is not healthy unless the margin is healthy.

The BI layer must always connect:

Sales
to
COGS
to
Gross Profit
to
Stock Loss
to
Discount Loss
to
True Profit

## COGS Components

SCI POS must support different cost components, including:

- Purchase cost
- Average cost
- Last purchase cost
- Landed cost
- Transport cost
- Duties and taxes where applicable
- Packaging cost
- Handling cost
- Branch transfer cost
- Wastage cost
- Write-off cost
- Return damage cost
- Supplier cost changes

## Product Cost Intelligence

Each product must show:

- Current cost
- Last purchase cost
- Average cost
- Selling price
- Gross profit
- Gross margin percentage
- Target margin
- Margin status
- Supplier cost trend
- Recommended selling price

Example:

Product: Cooking Oil 2L
Selling Price: 4.50
Average Cost: 3.80
Gross Profit: 0.70
Gross Margin: 15.5 percent
Target Margin: 25 percent
Status: Weak Margin

## Cost Change Detection

The BI layer must detect:

- Supplier cost increase
- Supplier cost decrease
- Cost increase without selling price update
- Product sold below cost
- Product sold below target margin
- Purchase price captured incorrectly
- Supplier invoice cost mismatch
- Branch transfer cost not included
- Landed cost not included

## Margin Erosion Alerts

The system must create alerts when:

- Product margin falls below target
- Product is sold below cost
- Supplier increases cost sharply
- Discounts reduce margin below safe level
- Purchase cost exceeds selling price
- Cost increases repeatedly in short period
- Product category margin is declining

Example:

COGS Alert

Product: Brake Pads Toyota
Current Selling Price: 18
True Cost: 16.80
Gross Margin: 6.7 percent
Target Margin: 25 percent
Recommendation: Increase selling price or renegotiate supplier cost.

## Supplier Cost Intelligence

COGS BI must connect supplier behavior to profit.

The system must show:

- Supplier cost movement
- Affected products
- Margin impact
- Estimated monthly profit loss
- Supplier reliability
- Supplier price stability

Example:

Supplier ABC increased prices by 18 percent in 60 days.
Affected Products: 23
Estimated Monthly Margin Loss: 1240
Recommendation: Compare alternate suppliers.

## COGS and Stock Theft Connection

COGS Intelligence must connect with stock theft BI.

Missing stock is not only a quantity problem.

It is a cost and profit problem.

Example:

Product: Battery 70AH
Missing Quantity: 3
Cost Loss: 180
Selling Value Exposure: 270
Gross Profit Damage: 90

The system must show how stock loss damages gross profit.

## Future Prediction Possibilities

SCI POS must be ready to predict:

- Margin collapse
- Products likely to become unprofitable
- Supplier cost risk
- Selling price update requirements
- Products that need price review
- Categories with declining profitability
- Branches selling at weak margins
- Staff discount behavior damaging profit

## Strategic Principle

COGS Intelligence must protect the vendor from the false comfort of high sales.

SCI POS must teach the business owner that revenue is not profit.

Profit is protected when cost, price, stock loss, discounts, and supplier behavior are controlled together.
