# SCI POS Business Intelligence Purchase Intelligence

## Purpose

Purchase Intelligence protects the business from uncontrolled buying, supplier cost shocks, overstocking, understocking, poor reorder discipline, and purchases that damage profitability.

A business can lose money before stock is even sold if purchases are poorly controlled.

SCI POS must help vendors buy the right products, from the right supplier, at the right cost, in the right quantity, at the right time.

## Core Principle

Purchasing must be driven by intelligence, not guesswork.

SCI POS must connect purchasing decisions to:

- Sales velocity
- Current stock
- Stockout risk
- Dead stock risk
- Supplier reliability
- Supplier cost trends
- Target margins
- Available cash
- Branch demand
- Seasonal demand

## Purchase Data Inputs

The BI layer must monitor:

- Purchase orders
- Purchase approvals
- Supplier invoices
- Goods received notes
- Cost price changes
- Quantity received
- Quantity ordered
- Purchase date
- Expected delivery date
- Actual delivery date
- Branch or warehouse receiving
- Staff who created purchase
- Staff who approved purchase
- Staff who received goods

## Purchase Discipline Checks

SCI POS must detect:

- Goods received without purchase order
- Purchase order without approval
- Supplier invoice mismatch
- Quantity ordered not matching quantity received
- Purchase cost above historical range
- Emergency purchases repeated too often
- Purchases not supported by sales velocity
- Overstocking of slow-moving items
- Under-ordering of fast-moving items
- Purchases that weaken margin

## Reorder Intelligence

SCI POS must recommend reorder quantities based on:

- Current stock
- Average daily sales
- Supplier lead time
- Safety stock
- Seasonal demand
- Branch demand
- Minimum stock level
- Maximum stock level
- Cash availability
- Product margin

Example:

Product: Cooking Oil 2L

Current Stock: 22

Average Daily Sales: 5

Supplier Lead Time: 7 days

Predicted Stockout: 4 days

Recommendation: Reorder immediately.

## Overstock Risk

The BI engine must detect:

- Slow-moving stock being reordered
- Dead stock being purchased again
- Products with low sales velocity but high purchase quantity
- Seasonal products bought at wrong time
- High-value products overstocked unnecessarily

Overstocking ties money in shelves and weakens cash flow.

## Understock Risk

The BI engine must detect:

- Fast-moving products with low stock cover
- Products likely to run out before supplier delivery
- Branches losing sales due to stockouts
- Products repeatedly out of stock
- Supplier delays affecting availability

## Purchase Cost Risk

SCI POS must detect:

- Supplier cost increases
- Abnormal purchase cost
- Purchase cost higher than selling price allows
- Products becoming unprofitable after purchase
- Cost captured incorrectly
- Supplier invoice price not matching purchase order

## Purchase Alerts

SCI POS must create alerts for:

- Purchase without approval
- Goods received without purchase order
- Supplier invoice mismatch
- Quantity mismatch
- Cost increase
- Overstock risk
- Stockout risk
- Reorder required
- Purchase damaging margin
- Emergency buying pattern

## Future Prediction Possibilities

SCI POS must be ready to predict:

- Products needing reorder soon
- Products likely to become overstocked
- Purchases likely to damage cash flow
- Supplier cost increases
- Branch demand changes
- Seasonal reorder requirements
- Purchase quantities needed by branch
- Slow-moving products that should not be reordered

## Strategic Principle

Purchase Intelligence must protect the business before money is spent.

A good POS does not only record what was bought.

SCI POS must guide what should be bought, when it should be bought, and whether the purchase will protect profit.
