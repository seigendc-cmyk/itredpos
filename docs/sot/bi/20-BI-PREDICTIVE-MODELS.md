# SCI POS Business Intelligence Predictive Models

## Purpose

Predictive Models allow SCI POS to warn the business before problems happen.

The first version of predictive intelligence can be rule-based. Advanced AI can come later.

The purpose is to help vendors prepare for future risks, future demand, future stockouts, future margin pressure, future customer loss, and future business decline.

## Core Principle

SCI POS must not only explain the past.

It must help predict what is likely to happen next.

Every prediction must include:

- Prediction
- Reason
- Confidence Level
- Business Impact
- Recommended Action

## Prediction Areas

SCI POS must support future prediction in:

- Stockouts
- Theft risk
- Stock variance
- Margin collapse
- COGS increase
- Supplier risk
- Customer churn
- Sales decline
- Branch decline
- Cash variance
- Delivery failure
- Staff risk escalation

## Stockout Prediction

The system must use:

- Current stock
- Average daily sales
- Supplier lead time
- Safety stock
- Seasonal demand
- Branch demand

Example:

Product: Cooking Oil 2L

Current Stock: 22

Average Daily Sales: 5

Predicted Stockout: 4 days

Recommendation:
Reorder immediately.

## Theft Risk Prediction

The system must predict theft or stock leakage risk using:

- Repeated variance
- High-value items
- Staff links
- Shelf risk
- Adjustment patterns
- Refund patterns
- Void patterns
- Delivery mismatch

Example:

Shelf B3 has high probability of variance based on previous shortages and high-value stock.

Recommendation:
Run surprise count today.

## Margin Collapse Prediction

The system must predict margin risk using:

- Supplier cost increases
- Selling price delay
- Discount activity
- Cost capture errors
- Landed cost changes

Example:

Supplier cost increased three times in 60 days.

Selling price unchanged.

Prediction:
Margin will fall below safe threshold.

Recommendation:
Review pricing.

## Customer Churn Prediction

The system must predict customer loss using:

- Last purchase date
- Normal purchase cycle
- Reduced basket size
- Reduced visit frequency
- Complaints
- Delivery disputes

## Branch Decline Prediction

The system must predict branch decline using:

- Sales trend
- Protected profit trend
- Stock variance trend
- Cash variance trend
- Customer churn
- Staff risk
- Delivery failure

## Future Model Governance

Predictions must be explainable.

SCI POS must not produce black-box advice without showing why.

Predictions must be treated as decision support, not absolute truth.

## Strategic Principle

Predictive BI must help vendors act early.

The best business intelligence is not the one that explains failure after it happens.

The best intelligence helps prevent failure before it happens.
