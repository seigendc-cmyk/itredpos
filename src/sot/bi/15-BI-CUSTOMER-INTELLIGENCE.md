# SCI POS Business Intelligence Customer Intelligence

## Purpose

Customer Intelligence helps vendors understand customer behavior, loyalty, value, risk, and future sales opportunities.

SCI POS must help ordinary vendors know who their customers are, what they buy, how often they buy, when they stop buying, and how customer behavior affects business growth.

Customer Intelligence must connect customer activity to sales, profit, stock planning, delivery performance, and retention.

## Core Principle

Customers are business assets.

SCI POS must help vendors protect and grow customer relationships through evidence-based intelligence.

## Customer Profile

Each customer profile should include:

- Customer Name
- Contact Details
- Customer Type
- Purchase History
- Total Spend
- Average Basket Value
- Purchase Frequency
- Last Purchase Date
- Preferred Products
- Preferred Branch
- Payment Behavior
- Delivery History
- Return History
- Complaint History
- Customer Value Score
- Churn Risk Score

## Customer Value Intelligence

SCI POS must classify customers by value.

Examples:

- New Customer
- Active Customer
- Regular Customer
- High-Value Customer
- Dormant Customer
- At-Risk Customer
- Lost Customer

Example:

Customer: ABC Construction
Total Spend: 42,000
Transactions: 128
Average Basket: 328
Status: High-Value Customer

## Customer Churn Detection

The BI layer must detect when customers are likely to stop buying.

Example:

Customer: XYZ Hardware
Last Purchase: 65 days ago
Normal Purchase Cycle: 14 days
Risk: High Churn Risk
Recommendation: Contact customer.

## Customer Buying Patterns

SCI POS must analyze:

- Products frequently bought
- Categories preferred
- Purchase cycle
- Average order size
- Branch preference
- Seasonal buying behavior
- Discount sensitivity
- Delivery preference

## Customer Profitability

Customer value must not only be based on sales.

The system must also calculate:

- Gross profit by customer
- Discount impact
- Return impact
- Delivery cost impact
- Net customer contribution

A customer with high sales but excessive returns or discounts may have weak profitability.

## Customer Risk Detection

SCI POS must detect:

- Repeated refunds
- Suspicious return patterns
- High discount dependency
- Delivery disputes
- Unpaid balances where credit is supported
- Customer linked to suspicious cashier activity
- Customer buying below safe margin

## Customer Alerts

SCI POS must create alerts for:

- High-value customer not buying
- Customer churn risk
- Repeated refund customer
- Customer complaint increase
- Customer delivery dispute
- Customer buying pattern change
- Customer profitability decline

## Customer Recommendations

Examples:

Observation:
ABC Construction has not bought for 45 days.

Risk:
Possible customer loss.

Recommendation:
Follow up with customer.

---

Observation:
Customer frequently buys cement and roofing sheets.

Recommendation:
Offer related hardware items.

## Future Prediction Possibilities

SCI POS must be ready to predict:

- Customer churn
- Customer lifetime value
- Next likely purchase
- Customer category demand
- Customer promotion response
- Customer delivery preference
- Customer profitability trend

## Strategic Principle

Customer Intelligence must help vendors move from selling randomly to managing customer relationships.

SCI POS must help vendors retain valuable customers, identify lost customers, and grow repeat business.
