# SCI POS Business Intelligence Supplier Intelligence

## Purpose

Supplier Intelligence evaluates how suppliers affect profitability, stock availability, stock integrity, purchasing efficiency, customer satisfaction, and overall business health.

Most businesses only evaluate suppliers based on price.

SCI POS must evaluate suppliers based on total business impact.

A cheap supplier that causes stockouts, poor quality, short deliveries, invoice disputes, and margin damage may be more expensive than a reliable supplier with slightly higher prices.

## Core Principle

Every supplier relationship affects:

- Profitability
- Stock Availability
- Customer Satisfaction
- Cash Flow
- Operational Efficiency
- Risk Exposure

Supplier Intelligence must continuously measure supplier performance.

---

## Supplier Profile

Each supplier must have a BI profile containing:

- Supplier Name
- Supplier Category
- Contact Information
- Active Products
- Purchase Volume
- Purchase Value
- Cost Trend
- Reliability Score
- Quality Score
- Delivery Score
- Risk Score
- Profit Impact Score

---

## Supplier Performance Metrics

SCI POS must calculate:

### Delivery Performance

- On-time delivery percentage
- Late delivery frequency
- Average delay duration
- Delivery consistency

### Quantity Accuracy

- Ordered quantity
- Delivered quantity
- Short deliveries
- Excess deliveries

### Invoice Accuracy

- Invoice mismatches
- Price discrepancies
- Quantity discrepancies
- Missing documentation

### Quality Performance

- Product returns
- Defective goods
- Damaged goods
- Customer complaints linked to supplier products

---

## Supplier Cost Intelligence

The BI layer must monitor:

- Cost changes
- Cost volatility
- Historical pricing
- Supplier price increases
- Supplier price decreases
- Cost impact on margin

Example:

Supplier: ABC Motors

Average Cost Increase:
12 percent over 90 days

Affected Products:
27

Estimated Monthly Margin Loss:
1,450

Recommendation:
Review alternative suppliers.

---

## Supplier Reliability Score

The system must calculate:

Supplier Reliability Score

Factors:

- Delivery performance
- Invoice accuracy
- Product quality
- Communication quality
- Cost stability
- Supply consistency

Score Range:

0-30 = Poor

31-60 = Average

61-80 = Good

81-100 = Excellent

---

## Supplier Risk Detection

SCI POS must detect:

- Repeated late deliveries
- Repeated short deliveries
- Excessive cost increases
- Invoice inconsistencies
- Poor quality products
- High return rates
- Frequent stockout contribution
- Supply interruptions
- Product authenticity concerns
- Dependency on a single supplier

---

## Supplier Dependency Risk

The BI engine must identify products that depend heavily on one supplier.

Example:

Battery 70AH

Supplier Dependency:
92 percent

Risk:
Critical

Recommendation:
Identify alternative suppliers.

Supplier concentration risk can create major business disruption.

---

## Supplier Impact on Profit

The system must show:

- Supplier contribution to revenue
- Supplier contribution to gross profit
- Supplier contribution to stockouts
- Supplier contribution to margin erosion
- Supplier contribution to inventory risk

The owner must understand which suppliers strengthen or weaken profitability.

---

## Supplier Alerts

SCI POS must create alerts for:

- Cost increase detected
- Repeated short delivery
- Repeated late delivery
- Invoice mismatch
- Margin impact detected
- Supplier reliability decline
- Supplier dependency risk
- Product quality issue
- Product return spike

---

## Supplier Recommendations

Examples:

Observation:
Supplier costs increased 18 percent.

Impact:
Margins reduced below target.

Recommendation:
Review pricing or alternative suppliers.

---

Observation:
Supplier delivered short quantities three times this month.

Risk:
Receiving integrity issue.

Recommendation:
Increase receiving verification procedures.

## Future Prediction Possibilities

SCI POS must predict:

- Supplier cost increases
- Supplier delivery risk
- Future stockout risk
- Supplier reliability decline
- Margin pressure caused by suppliers
- Supplier dependency exposure
- Supplier replacement opportunities

---

## Strategic Principle

Suppliers are strategic business assets.

SCI POS must help vendors understand not only who they buy from, but how each supplier affects profit, stock availability, business risk, and long-term growth.

The best supplier is not necessarily the cheapest supplier.

The best supplier is the one that protects business performance.
